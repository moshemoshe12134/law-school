### Questions Harcourt Actually Asks

**Type 1: "What does the statute say?"**
- He wants you to READ the statute aloud or identify the verbs/elements
- Example: "What are the acts in this codification?" (asked of Eli, Class 2)

**Type 2: "What would prosecution argue?"**
- Wants the STRONGEST prosecution argument, with MPC cite
- Example: "You rest on the boisterousness. That's voluntary. Period." (Class 2)

**Type 3: "Defense counsel, respond"**
- Wants you to identify the voluntariness/mens rea weakness
- Example: "Appearance involuntary because police arrested him" (Rabia, Class 2)

**Type 4: "What does [mens rea term] mean?"**
- Wants translation to MPC categories (purpose/knowledge/recklessness/negligence)
- Example: "What does 'malicious' mean here?" â†’ student must map to recklessness (Class 3)

**Type 5: "How do you distinguish [Case A] from [Case B]?"**
- Wants the critical factual/doctrinal difference
- Not asked yet in Classes 1-3, but expected based on exam format

**Type 6: Time-framing challenge**
- "If you expand the time frame..." or "What if we narrow the frame..."
- Tests whether you see Kelman's point about framing affecting outcome

### What Harcourt Does NOT Do

- âŒ Ask detailed fact-recall questions ("What color was the car?")
- âŒ Ask you to recite case holdings verbatim
- âŒ Quiz on dates, jurisdictions, or procedural history
- âŒ Expect memorized MPC text (you have books open)
