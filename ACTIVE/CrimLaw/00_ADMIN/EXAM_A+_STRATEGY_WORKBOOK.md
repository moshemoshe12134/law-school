## Exam Format Analysis

### Consistent Harcourt Format (2006-2023)

**Three-Question Structure:**
1. **Q1: Issue-Spotter Fact Pattern** (~50% of grade, ~1200-1500 words)
   - Apply MPC as BOTH prosecutor AND defense
   - Role-play format: memo to judge, prosecutor, or trial court
   - Real-world cases or complex hypotheticals
   
2. **Q2: Policy/Theory Essay** (~25-30% of grade, ~1000-1250 words)
   - Synthesize theory readings (Foucault, Kelman, Stevenson, Steiker & Steiker)
   - Apply critical theory to criminal law doctrine
   - Often asks about punishment theory, knowledge/power, or justice
   
3. **Q3: Reflective/Professional Essay** (~20-25% of grade)
   - Personal reflection on justice, being a lawyer, course impact
   - Often connects to career path (prosecutor, defender, etc.)

### Time Allocation (4-hour exam)

| Section | Time | Words |
|---------|------|-------|
| Q1 Issue-Spotter | 90-100 min | 1200-1500 |
| Q2 Policy Essay | 60-75 min | 1000-1250 |
| Q3 Reflective | 45-60 min | 750-1000 |
| **Total** | 240 min | 2400-2500 max |

## Point-Earning Patterns

### Points Harcourt ALWAYS Rewards

1. **Â§3.09 Analysis for Justification Cases**
   - From 2018 Memo: The ENTIRE analysis often comes down to Â§3.09
   - Was belief formed recklessly or negligently?
   - This is where the grade is made or lost

2. **Distinguishing Recklessness from Negligence**
   - From 2018 Memo: "I don't believe the prosecution will charge manslaughter here, because I think it will be extremely difficult to prove and convict based upon a recklessness mens rea. Here, however, negligence is a possibility."
   - Shows you understand the difference matters

3. **Acknowledging What You CAN'T Prove**
   - Good answers say "The prosecution cannot establish..." when appropriate
   - Shows realistic prosecutorial judgment

4. **Attempt Requires Purpose**
   - From 2019 Memo: "The central question is whether there can be an attempted manslaughter under the MPC: does the MPC provision on attempt allow a charge where the mens rea is recklessness or negligence? Under MPC Â§ 5.01(1)(b) requires purpose."
   - This is a favorite Harcourt point

5. **Time-Framing (Kelman)**
   - Referenced in almost every model answer
   - Narrow vs. broad time frame for actus reus
   - Always mention when voluntariness is at issue

6. **Policy Context**
   - From 2018 Memo: "the court will need to take into consideration the context of police shootings today and the racial dynamics at play"
   - Harcourt rewards awareness of systemic issues

### Points That Get Ignored (Don't Waste Time)

1. **Obvious Causation** â€” Unless genuinely contested, one sentence max
2. **Basic Actus Reus** â€” Unless involuntariness is the issue
3. **EED/Insanity When Not Implicated** â€” From 2019 Memo: "this case does not raise EED or insanity, so do not waste any time on those"
4. **Extended Discussion of Obvious Mens Rea** â€” If someone shoots with intent, don't belabor it

### Surprising Point-Earners

   - Not just guilty/not-guilty but what level of culpability survives
   
2. **Differentiating Defendants**
   - From 2018 Memo: "in terms of differentiating defendants, you could argue that officer David, who only fired once, was less negligent"
