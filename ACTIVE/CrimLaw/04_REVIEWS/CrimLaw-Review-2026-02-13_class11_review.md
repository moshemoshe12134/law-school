## 4. DOCTRINAL INSIGHTS

### The "Regulation of Torture" (Not Abolition)
Harcourt reframed the exclusion of confessions not as "anti-torture" but as "regulated torture."
- **Roman Law (Justinian)**: You can torture slaves, BUT you must follow rules (e.g., no leading questions like "Why did you poison him?").
- **Alabama Law**: You can't use the confession if the master is present or if it's a "leading question" situation (Clarissa's overseer assuming guilt).
- **Insight**: The legal system's goal was not to stop pain, but to ensure the *reliability* of the pain-extracted truth so the system (slavery) wouldn't collapse under false accusations.

### Economic Incentives of Due Process
- **The 50% Rule**: State paid 50% of executed slave's value to owner.
- **Governor's Veto**: Vetoed bill for 100% reimbursement.
- **Why?** If owners got 100%, they'd dump bad saves by accusing them of capital crimes. "Humanity alone... is the only inducement."
- **Doctrine**: "Humanity" (due process) is actually an economic regulatory mechanism to prevent market failure (owners destroying their own capital at state expense).

## 5. POLICY ARGUMENTS

### The "Legitimation" Argument (Stabilization)
- **Argument**: "This procedural protection [exclusion of confession] actually *strengthens* the unjust system."
- **Why**: It prevents the system from becoming too chaotic/brutal (which would cause revolt or economic loss). It creates an "equilibrium" that allows the oppressive social order to persist.
- **Harcourt's Phrasing**: "Negotiating the space of slavery so that it can continue to survive."

### The "Formalism as Complicity" Argument (Cover)
- **Argument**: "By separating specific legal questions (indictment sufficiency) from the broader moral reality (slavery), the court acts as Captain Vere."
- **Application**: The judge absolves themself of responsibility ("I just apply the law") while serving as the essential machinery of the injustice. Law serves to *distance* the moral actor from their action.

### The "Truth Production" Argument (Foucault)
- **Argument**: "Legal truth is not what happened; it's what the procedure produces."
- **Evidence**:
    - **Homer**: Truth = outcome of the Oath Ritual (risk).
    - **Clarissa**: Truth = outcome of the Review Procedure (technicalities).
    - **Modern**: Truth = outcome of the Plea Bargain/Trial rules.
- **Use**: Attack a "fact" on the exam by attacking the *procedure* that produced it.

## 8. PREWRITE OPPORTUNITIES

### The "Captain Vere" Prewrite
**Trigger**: A judge in a hypo expresses personal discomfort with a ruling but says "the statute compels me."
**Response**:
1.  Identify the **Formalist/Positivist** stance (Law is distinct from Morals).
2.  Critique using **Cover/Vere**: By retreating to "office" ("these buttons"), the judge becomes complicit in the substantive injustice.
3.  Counter with **Instrumentalist/Realist** view: The judge *constructs* the law; they could interpret the "noxious weed" ambiguous statute differently if they wanted to avoid the injustice (like the court in *Clarissa* finding a technicality to *save* the defendant, though here it was to save the *system*).
