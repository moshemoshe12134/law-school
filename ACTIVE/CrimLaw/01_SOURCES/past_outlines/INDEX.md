## Topic Index

### 1. ACTUS REUS (Classes 2-3)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key sections:**
- MPC 1.13(2), 1.13(9), 2.01 definitions
- *Martin v. State* (involuntary appearance)
- *People v. Newton* (unconsciousness)
- *People v. Decina* (seizure while driving)
- Omissions (Jones duties)
- Time framing (Kelman)

**Exam patterns:** Often tested via hypo where D claims act was involuntary. Key: when to start the "time frame."

---

### 2. MENS REA (Classes 3-4)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key sections:**
- MPC 2.02 culpability hierarchy
- Purpose vs. knowledge vs. recklessness vs. negligence
- *Regina v. Cunningham* (recklessness)
- *Hazelwood* vs. *Santillanes* (distinguishing recklessness/negligence)
- *Elonis* (knowledge of threat)

**Exam patterns:** Element analysis — which mental state applies? Default rules under MPC.

---

### 3. HOMICIDE (Classes 4-8)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Sub-topics:**

#### 3a. Intended Killings / Premeditation
- *Carroll* (premeditation in instant)
- *Guthrie* (requires meaningful reflection)
- MPC 210.2, 210.3

#### 3b. Heat of Passion / Voluntary Manslaughter
- *Girouard* (words alone insufficient)
- *Maher* (objective reasonable person)
- *Casassa* (extreme emotional disturbance under MPC)

#### 3c. Felony Murder
- *Regina v. Serné* (inherently dangerous)
- *People v. Stamp* (proximate cause)
- *State v. Canola* (merger doctrine)
- Limitations: inherently dangerous, merger, res gestae

#### 3d. Unintended Killings
- *Commonwealth v. Malone* (Russian roulette)
- *Welansky* (depraved heart)
- *Hall* (ski slope recklessness)
- *Williams* (negligent homicide)

#### 3e. Causation
- *Acosta* (proximate cause)
- *Arzon* (intervening causes)
- MPC 2.03

**Exam patterns:** Usually a hypo with death → categorize as murder 1, murder 2, voluntary manslaughter, involuntary, or negligent homicide. Apply both common law AND MPC.

---

### 4. THEORIES OF PUNISHMENT (Classes 9-11)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key frameworks:**
- Retribution (desert, proportionality)
- Utilitarianism (deterrence, incapacitation, rehabilitation)
- Foucault (Discipline and Punish)
- Critical theory / abolition

**Exam patterns:** Policy essay question. Must synthesize theory with doctrine. Original argument required.

---

### 5. LIMITS ON PUNISHMENT / LEGALITY (Classes 12-13)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key sections:**
- *Keeler* (legality principle, rule of lenity)
- Vagueness doctrine (*Morales v. Chicago*)
- Status crimes (*Robinson v. California*)

**Exam patterns:** Constitutional limits on what can be criminalized.

---

### 6. CAPITAL PUNISHMENT (Classes 14-15)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key sections:**
- *Gregg v. Georgia* (death penalty constitutional with procedures)
- *Atkins v. Virginia* (intellectual disability)
- *McCleskey v. Kemp* (racial disparities)

**Exam patterns:** Policy essay on death penalty, race, proportionality.

---

### 7. SEXUAL ASSAULT (Classes 16-17)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Key sections:**
- *State v. Rusk* (force requirement)
- *State v. DiPetrillo*
- *State in Interest of M.T.S.* (affirmative consent)
- *Commonwealth v. Sherry*

**Exam patterns:** Consent analysis, force vs. affirmative consent models.

---

### 8. JUSTIFICATIONS & EXCUSES (Classes 19-23)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Sub-topics:**

#### 8a. Self-Defense
- *U.S. v. Peterson* (initial aggressor, duty to retreat)
- MPC 3.04, 3.09
- Battered women's syndrome (*State v. Norman*)

#### 8b. Duty to Retreat / Stand Your Ground
- Castle doctrine
- Stand your ground laws
- Race and self-defense

#### 8c. Law Enforcement Use of Force
- *Tennessee v. Garner*
- MPC 3.07
- Police shootings

#### 8d. Necessity & Duress
- *Regina v. Dudley & Stephens*
- *People v. Unger* (prison escape)
- *State v. Toscano* (duress)
- MPC 3.02, 2.09

#### 8e. Insanity
- M'Naghten (right/wrong)
- MPC 4.01
- Diminished capacity

**Exam patterns:** Hypo with D claiming justification/excuse. Apply both common law AND MPC. Often combined with self-defense + retreat.

---

### 9. EXPANDING LIABILITY (Classes 24-26)

**Best source:** `Crim_Spring 2025_Harcourt_Full.docx.md`

**Sub-topics:**

#### 9a. Attempt
- MPC 5.01
- Substantial step test
- Abandonment

#### 9b. Complicity / Accomplice Liability
- MPC 2.06
- Purpose to promote/facilitate
- Natural and probable consequences

#### 9c. Conspiracy
- MPC 5.03
- Pinkerton liability
- Bilateral vs. unilateral

**Exam patterns:** Multiple defendants → who is liable for what? Apply accomplice + conspiracy liability.
