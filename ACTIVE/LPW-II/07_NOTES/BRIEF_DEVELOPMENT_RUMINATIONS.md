## üéØ Theory of the Case (Copyright Issue)

### Central Narrative

> "This case asks whether fair use protects transformative educational criticism that corrects historical inaccuracies. It does."

### EchoCraft's Story (Copyright)

**Riven's song contained historical inaccuracies ‚Üí EchoCraft corrected them ‚Üí Public benefits from accurate education**

### Three Dimensions of Transformation (from outline)

1. **Changed Purpose:**
   - Original: Celebrate Revere as "great man"
   - EchoCraft: Question whether Revere should be revered

2. **Changed Message:**
   - Original: Revere acted alone, "revealed no secrets"
   - EchoCraft: Multiple riders (Dawes, Prescott, Ludington), Revere "revealed some secrets," was "skittish"

3. **Changed Pedagogical Function:**
   - Original: Memorization tool for passive learning
   - EchoCraft: Critical thinking prompt soliciting "critical discussion"

### Key Symbolic Element
- **Question mark in title**: "Revere Paul Revere?" vs. "Revere Paul Revere"
- Physical manifestation of critical inquiry

## ¬©Ô∏è Issue 2: Copyright / Fair Use (YOUR ISSUE)

### The Numbers (Know These Cold)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Riven's original | 66 words (8 lines) |
| EchoCraft's song | 116 words (14 lines) |
| Words copied verbatim | 57 words |
| % of Riven's work copied | **86.4%** (57/66) |
| Copied words as % of final | 49.1% (57/116) |
| New original content | 59 words (50.9%) |
| Length increase | 75.8% (66 ‚Üí 116) |
| Licensing fee | $200 (at/below cost) |

### Factor-by-Factor Summary

| Factor | Direction | Weight | Key Point |
|--------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **1. Purpose/Character** | ‚úÖ STRONGLY FOR | HIGH | Transformative criticism + educational + nonprofit |
| **2. Nature** | ‚ö†Ô∏è Slightly against | LOW | Creative work but factual content; *Campbell* says minimal weight |
| **3. Amount** | ‚ö†Ô∏è Needs justification | MEDIUM | 86.4% copied BUT necessary for criticism; added 50% new content |
| **4. Market Effect** | ‚úÖ FOR | HIGH | No established market; only preliminary negotiation |

### Factor 1: Purpose & Character (STRONGEST)

**Why we win here:**
- Transformative = "adds something new, with further purpose or different character" (*Campbell*)
- Falls within statutory categories: "criticism, comment... teaching" (¬ß 107)
- Nonprofit educational distribution through EchoCraft Ed
- Licensed at/below cost ($200)

**Post-*Warhol* concern:**
- *Warhol* restricts same-market uses: "if original work and secondary use share same or highly similar purposes, and secondary use is commercial, first factor likely weighs against fair use"
- **BUT:** *Warhol* focuses on PURPOSE not market category
- Our purpose is fundamentally different (critical inquiry vs. celebration)

**Key distinction:**
- Market category (education) ‚â† Market purpose (memorization vs. critical thinking)

### Factor 2: Nature of Work (WEAKEST BUT LOW WEIGHT)

**Why it's against us:**
- Song is original creative work (lyrics, rhyme scheme)
- Published work with registered copyright

**Why it doesn't matter much:**
- *Campbell*: Factor 2 "not likely to help much" for creative works
- Much of content is factual (historical events, dates, famous phrases)
- *Feist*: Facts receive NO copyright protection
- Informational works get less protection than purely creative

### Factor 3: Amount & Substantiality (NEEDS WORK)

**The problem:**
- 86.4% of original copied (57 of 66 words)
- Only 4.3% approved in *Maxtone-Graham* (highest 2d Cir. for criticism)

**The justification:**
- *Campbell*: May copy enough to "conjure up" the original
- Cannot correct inaccuracies without reproducing them
- Must establish traditional narrative before questioning it
- Added equal amount of new content (59 words = 50.9% of final)
- Final work is 75.8% longer than original

**Key question to resolve:**
> Could the critical purpose have been achieved with less copying?

**Our answer:** No‚Äîcorrections require showing what is being corrected. Cannot write "Revere revealed some secrets" without context of original's "revealed no secrets."

### Factor 4: Market Effect (STRONG)

**Why we win here:**
- Only ONE preliminary negotiation (Toobin/Columbia Grammar)
- No signed contract
- No agreed-upon price
- $500 was "preliminary negotiation fee," not licensing payment
- "We'll talk more soon" = ongoing discussion, not agreement

**Key legal standard (*Castle Rock*):**
- Factor 4 focuses on "traditional, reasonable, or likely-to-be-developed markets" NOT speculative opportunities
- *TCA Television*: Requires "regular" requests "frequently" granted‚Äînot isolated discussions

**Market superiority vs. substitution:**
- Toobin chose EchoCraft because it "solicits critical discussion"
- Choice based on educational superiority, not mere duplication
- *ASCAP*: Harm from criticism that "lowered public's estimation of original" = FAIR USE

### ASSUMPTION 1: "Transformative Use" Post-Warhol

**What I'm assuming:**
- That educational criticism in the same market (AP History) can still be "transformative" post-Warhol
- That adding a question mark + correcting facts = sufficient transformation

**What I need to prove:**
- [ ] Post-Warhol 2d Cir. cases finding transformation despite same market
- [ ] What degree of purpose change overcomes market overlap?
- [ ] Does Warhol require transformation to be SO strong it overcomes market similarity?
- [ ] Is "educational market" too broad a category? (Both target AP History students)

**The danger:**
- Warhol: "if original work and secondary use share same or highly similar purposes... first factor likely weighs against fair use"
- Both works teach AP History students
- Appellant will argue: both serve pedagogical purpose ‚Üí same purpose under Warhol

### ASSUMPTION 2: "Criticism" Includes Factual Correction

**What I'm assuming:**
- That correcting historical inaccuracies = "criticism" under ¬ß 107
- That ¬ß 107's "criticism" covers substantive content, not just meta-commentary

**What I need to prove:**
- [ ] Cases where factual correction qualified as statutory "criticism"
- [ ] Is there a distinction between criticizing HOW something is written vs. WHAT it says?
- [ ] Does "criticism" require targeting the work's style/expression, or can it target its substance?
- [ ] Warhol's "critical bearing" requirement - does factual correction have "critical bearing on substance"?

**The danger:**
- Courts might say: You're not criticizing Riven's SONG (expression), you're teaching different historical facts
- That's not criticism of the WORK, that's using history for your own pedagogical purposes

### ASSUMPTION 3: Nonprofit Structure Proves Nonprofit Purpose

**What I'm assuming:**
- That EchoCraft Ed (subsidiary) being nonprofit = fair use gets nonprofit benefit
- That at-cost pricing proves educational purpose
- That incidental parent company benefits don't matter

**What I need to prove:**
- [ ] Cases analyzing parent-subsidiary nonprofit structures in fair use
- [ ] What's the "primary purpose" test? (Mayo Clinic case cited but need 2d Cir.)
- [ ] At what point do reputational/revenue benefits to parent make use "commercial"?
- [ ] Does revenue improvement (-5% ‚Üí +2%) after release create inference of commercial motive?

**The danger:**
- Appellant will say: EchoCraft Ed is a shell; real purpose was PR for parent company
- Revenue correlation suggests commercial motive
- "Below cost" might still be profitable if production costs are low

### ASSUMPTION 4: 86.4% Copying Can Be Justified

**What I'm assuming:**
- That "conjure up" principle allows copying 86.4% for criticism
- That necessity justifies this high percentage
- That adding 50% new content demonstrates restraint

- [ ] Highest approved percentage was 4.3% (Maxtone-Graham) - how to distinguish?
- [ ] Is there a de facto ceiling on criticism copying?
- [ ] Does Campbell's "heart" language apply to factual educational works?

**The danger:**
- This is possibly a FATAL problem
- 86.4% is astronomically higher than any approved 2d Circuit case
- Courts might say: You could have made your point with less
- "Conjure up" might require only enough to identify the work, not reproduce most of it

### ASSUMPTION 5: Preliminary Negotiation ‚â† "Potential Market"

**What I'm assuming:**

## üéØ Anticipated Counter-Arguments & Responses

### Counter 1: "Minimal Transformation"
**Appellant:** Just adding question mark and few lines isn't transformative; 86.4% copying shows lack of transformation

**Response:** Transformation measured by change in purpose/message, not percentage of new content. Question format + corrected facts + new lines = fundamentally different purpose.

### Counter 2: "Commercial Exploitation Disguised as Education"
**Appellant:** Nonprofit division is sham; PR benefits and revenue improvement show commercial purpose

**Response:** Primary purpose test looks to actual distribution mechanism and pricing. Incidental benefits don't defeat primary educational purpose (*Hachette*).

### Counter 3: "Excessive, Unnecessary Copying"
**Appellant:** 86.4% is per se excessive; could have conveyed same information without copying lyrics

**Response:** Reasonableness, not minimization, is standard. Purpose was to criticize Riven's SPECIFIC claims‚Äîrequires reproducing them. Added 50% new content.

### Counter 4: "Direct Market Substitution"
**Appellant:** Toobin literally chose EchoCraft instead of Riven‚Äîparadigmatic substitution

**Response:** Substitution vs. superiority. Toobin chose better educational tool. No contract existed‚Äîpreliminary negotiations don't establish market.

### Counter 5: "Factual Disputes Preclude Summary Judgment"
**Appellant:** Whether use is transformative is factual question for jury

**Response:** Fair use determination is legal question. "Is this transformative?" = legal characterization, not fact-finding.
