# Statement of Facts - Riven v. EchoCraft (Retaliation Claim)

## SHORT VERSION (Chronological Summary)

**September 2018:** Riven hired as songwriter at EchoCraft. (Record p.13)

**February 2020:** Riven promoted to Manager of country songwriting department after ~18 months—unusually fast. (Record p.13, 41)

**2021-2022:** Riven receives "Exceeding Expectations" reviews; department exceeds quotas. (Record p.14, 89-90)

**December 21, 2022:** Holiday party. Seven employees discuss bonuses; express concern Moses's department (mostly women: 12 women, 3 men) receiving lower bonuses than Riven's department (10 men, 5 women) despite comparable reviews. Employee suggests gender discrimination. Riven says he will investigate. (Record p.19-22)

**January 10, 2023:** Riven meets Sniff about bonus disparities. Sniff testifies concern framed as "departmental issue, not gender one." Riven testifies he mentioned "Jackie's department having a solid number of women" and "certainly felt that might be the cause." Sniff tells Riven he is "free to poke around." (Record p.23, 44)

**January-May 2023:** Riven interviews approximately 81 employees across 12 departments regarding bonuses. Department fails to meet quotas for six months. (Record p.23-24)

**May 24, 2023:** CEO Raine sends email to Sniff calling Riven "a virus" with "vendetta"; instructs Sniff to "keep your subordinates in line." Raine heard investigation concerned bonus disparities and "mention of Riven particularly focusing on departments with a lot of women." (Record p.68-69, Exhibit F)

**Late June/Early July 2023:** Three songwriters transferred from Riven's department on short notice (15 to 12 staff). (Record p.18-19, 51)

**July 5, 2023:** Sniff issues Riven formal warning for failing to meet quotas. Moses—with same performance issues but 20+ years more experience and 12+ years longer as manager—receives NO warning. (Record p.17, 48-49, Exhibit B)

**August 18, 2023:** Riven submits report finding that in 9 of 12 departments, women awarded lower bonuses than men. (Record p.25, Exhibit D)

**August 19, 2023:** Sniff responds: "I take these concerns very seriously. However, are you sure this is the best use of your time right now?" (Record p.93, Exhibit D)

**November 2023:** Sniff decides Riven "no longer in our future plans." (Record p.50)

**December 2023:** Sniff and Raine make "final determination" to terminate Riven for failing to meet quotas. (Record p.50, 67)

**December 21, 2023:** Riven terminated for "unsatisfactory performance." (Record p.96, Exhibit E)

**Key Comparator:** Moses had identical performance issues but was not warned or terminated. She had 20+ years more experience, 12+ years longer as manager, proven track record of recovery, and declined to participate in investigation. Her department met quotas Q4 2023; Riven's did not. (Record p.49, 60-63)

**Timeline Gap:** 7 months between report submission and termination; 4 months between report and termination decision.

## LONG VERSION (Detailed Facts)

### Background & Performance History

**September 2018:** Riven hired at EchoCraft as songwriter in country music department. (Record p.13)

**February 2020:** Riven promoted to Manager of country songwriting department after only ~18 months—unusually fast promotion. (Record p.13, 41)

**2021-2022:** Riven receives "Exceeding Expectations" performance reviews; called a "powerhouse"; department exceeds 25 songs/quarter quota. (Exhibit A; Record p.14, 89-90)

### Investigation Begins

**December 21, 2022:** Holiday party. Seven employees (3 men from Riven's dept, 4 women from Moses's dept) discuss bonuses and express concern that Moses's department receiving lower bonuses despite comparable performance reviews. One employee suggests gender discrimination as cause. Moses's department composition: 12 women, 3 men; Riven's department: 10 men, 5 women. Riven tells employees he will "look into it." (Record p.19-22; Complaint ¶11)

**January 10, 2023:** Riven meets with Sniff to express concerns about bonus disparities between his department and Moses's department. 
- **Sniff's testimony:** "his concern was a departmental issue, not a gender one"; "I think he used the phrase 'the women in Jackie's department' when he brought their concerns to me, but to me, it just seemed like he was interested in seeing if some departments were seeing higher bonuses than others." (Record p.44)
- **Riven's testimony (cross):** "I wasn't certain it was gender based, but I did mention something to Daphne about Jackie's department having a solid number of women. I certainly felt that might be the cause." (Record p.23)
- **Riven's testimony (redirect):** "Yes, gender was certainly on my mind given the makeup of our departments"; "I'm not sure what phraseology I used, but I think Daphne definitely understood that those were my concerns." (Record p.32)
- **Sniff's response:** Told Riven she didn't believe EchoCraft discriminated in assigning bonuses, but he was "free to poke around." (Record p.23, 44)

**January-May 2023:** Riven conducts investigation, interviewing approximately 81 employees across 12 departments in songwriting, marketing, and talent acquisition divisions regarding bonuses, performance reviews, and general workplace treatment. (Record p.23-24; Complaint ¶14)

### Performance Decline & Employer Response

**January-June 2023:** Riven's department fails to meet songwriting quotas for six consecutive months. (Record p.15-17; Exhibit B)

**May 24, 2023:** CEO Ryan Raine sends email to Sniff (Exhibit F):
- Context: Raine overheard two employees in advertising division discussing Riven being on "some sort of vendetta against the company through some massive investigation" about "people getting paid less in bonus than they were due." (Record p.68)
- Raine heard "some mention of Riven particularly focusing on departments with a lot of women, but that didn't mean much to me at the time." (Record p.68-69)
- Email states Riven "is on a vendetta against the firm"; calls him "a virus infecting the company culture"; instructs Sniff to "keep your subordinates in line" and ensure nothing affects "our bottom line." (Exhibit F; Record p.69)
- Raine never responded to this email or discussed investigation with Sniff again until June 2023. (Record p.75-76)

**Late June/Early July 2023:** Three of Riven's best songwriters transferred from his department (reducing staff from 15 to 12) on short notice. Sniff's explanation: needed for Pop department that was "growing at a much faster pace than we anticipated." (Record p.18-19, 51)

**July 5, 2023:** Sniff issues Riven formal warning for department's failure to meet quotas for six consecutive months. (Exhibit B; Record p.17, 48)

**July 5, 2023:** Sniff also meets with Jackie Moses, whose educational songwriting department similarly failed to meet quotas for six months. Moses does NOT receive formal warning. 
- **Moses background:** Hired 2000 (20 years before Riven); manager since 2008 (12 years longer than Riven); formed educational songwriting department; "proven track record" of recovering from performance dips. (Record p.49, 60-62)
- **Sniff's explanation:** Moses had experience and track record; "I had many fewer concerns with her than I did with Philip." (Record p.49)

### Report Submission

**August 18, 2023:** Riven emails report to Sniff. Report finds that in 9 out of 12 departments surveyed, women awarded lower average bonuses than men in 2022. Report also notes positive findings about EchoCraft's sexual harassment policies, parental leave, and time-off policies. (Exhibit D; Record p.25, 93-95)

**August 19, 2023:** Sniff responds: "I take these concerns very seriously. However, are you sure this is the best use of your time right now?" (Exhibit D; Record p.93)

**June-December 2023:** Riven's department continues to underperform quotas; produces under 20 songs per quarter (below 25/quarter expectation). (Exhibit C; Record p.17-18)

### Termination Decision

**November 2023:** Sniff "was pretty sure Philip was no longer in our future plans." (Record p.50)

**December 2023:** Sniff makes "final determination with Mr. Raine" to terminate Riven. (Record p.50)
- **Sniff's testimony:** Decision based "solely because he had not hit his quotas for the last year, and I had seen little improvement since I issued a formal warning." Investigation was not "one of the causes of his termination." (Record p.49)
- **Raine's testimony:** Sniff brought issue to his attention in December 2023 that "Riven's department had failed to meet their quotas the last 12 months consecutively." Raine deferred to Sniff's recommendation; had "no information suggesting a contrary course of action might be appropriate." (Record p.67)

**December 19, 2023:** HR Director Blaire Sinenski meets with Riven, informs him he is being terminated for underperformance. (Record p.26)

**December 21, 2023:** Riven receives termination letter stating termination for "unsatisfactory performance and a consistent failure to meet your department's quotas for songwriting credits." (Exhibit E; Record p.96)

### Key Comparator Evidence (Moses)

- **Experience differential:** Moses 20+ years more experience at company (2000 vs. 2018); 12+ years longer as manager (2008 vs. 2020)
- **Track record:** Moses created educational dept; has history of performance fluctuations and recovery ("every few years there tends to be a dip"; "we always bounce back") (Record p.61)
- **2023 recovery:** Moses's department met quota Q4 2023; Riven's did not. (Record p.61)
- **Moses's attitude:** "I started this department. I'm an institution... I was never really concerned about losing my job." (Record p.62-63)
- **Investigation participation:** Moses declined to participate in Riven's investigation: "You don't last this long as a woman in the music business by getting involved in stuff like this." (Record p.62)

### Timeline Summary

- **Dec 21, 2022:** Investigation genesis (holiday party)
- **Jan 10, 2023:** First meeting with Sniff (ambiguous framing)
- **Jan-May 2023:** Investigation conducted
- **May 24, 2023:** Raine "virus" email
- **July 5, 2023:** Formal warning (Moses gets none)
- **Aug 18, 2023:** Report submitted (clear gender claim)
- **Dec 21, 2023:** Termination
- **Time gap:** 7 months between report and termination; 4 months between report and termination decision
