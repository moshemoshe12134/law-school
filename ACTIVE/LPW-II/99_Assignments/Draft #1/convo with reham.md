### **Factor 3: Amount & Substantiality**

**Manageable ‚Äî lean on Santos and Campbell**

| Point                                | Detail                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Santos precedent**           | Even 100% of a work can be used if the purpose is sufficiently different ‚Äî Santos took an entire video and the court found fair use                                                                 |
| **Campbell precedent**         | Using the "heart of the work" is acceptable when necessary for the audience to recognize what's being parodied                                                                                       |
| **Qualitative > Quantitative** | Emphasize**qualitative** analysis over quantitative. Yes, 6 lines were copied. But 7 new lines were added, the meaning was completely reversed (hero ‚Üí traitor), and the core message changed |
| **Necessity argument**         | Echocraft HAD to use recognizable elements ‚Äî you can't critique something if the audience doesn't know what you're critiquing. Parody requires enough borrowing for identification                  |
| **Campbell language**          | "What makes for this recognition is quotation of the original's most distinctive and memorable features, which the parodist can be sure the audience will know"                                      |

**The line from Campbell:**

- **Floor:** You must use enough for the audience to recognize the original
- **Ceiling:** You cannot use so much that you're simply substituting for the original

### **Factor 4: Market Effect**

**Your weakest factor ‚Äî this is the battleground**

| Point                               | Detail                                                                                                           |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The problem**               | Echocraft's song directly replaced Riven's with the same principal at the same school. Clear market substitution |
| **Riven's argument**          | "I lost the deal with this school, and I would have expanded to other schools. You swallowed my market."         |
| **AI cases analogy**          | NYT v. OpenAI ‚Äî content creators are winning on market substitution theory. Dangerous precedent for us          |

**Your counter-arguments:**

1. **Parody creates its own market (Campbell):** In a parody, you're not encroaching on the original's market because your customers want something the original creator would never sell ‚Äî **criticism of their own product**. Riven is not in the business of critiquing his own song.
2. **Different audiences:** Students seeking memorization aids (Riven's market) ‚â† students seeking to question historical narratives (Echocraft's market). Like Campbell: rap listeners ‚â† pop listeners.
3. **Coexistence argument:** Both songs can coexist ‚Äî they show two different narratives. One is monolithic hero-worship; the other teaches nuance.
4. **Non-proximate cause:** Any indirect PR benefit to Echocraft (5% profit increase) is not proximately caused by the song. They worked on other songs. You can't claim damages for non-direct market effects.
5. **Author's Guild v. Google:** Google took 100% of books but didn't usurp the reading market ‚Äî it indexed. Similarly, Echocraft isn't replacing the memorization function; it's serving a different function (critical thinking).

**Unresolved challenge you flagged:**

- How does parody work when most of the audience has never heard the original? This is a genuine research gap you identified. You noted Campbell had the same issue but the court didn't seem troubled by it.
