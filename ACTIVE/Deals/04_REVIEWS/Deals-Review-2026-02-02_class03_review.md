## KEY CONCEPTS (From Prep - Reinforced in Class)

### Core "Deals" Moves

**As applied to residential real estate:**

1. **Information asymmetry:** Seller knows more about defects/history
   - **Tools:** DD, walk-away rights, reps + remedies, survival clauses

2. **Uncertainty:** Some things can't be known until later
   - **Tools:** Contingent purchase price, escrow/holdback, spring testing rights

3. **Incentives / moral hazard:** 
   - If Seller fixes, may do it cheaply
   - If Buyer "insured," may underinvest in care
   - **Tools:** Third-party certification, scope of work requirements, professional standards

### "Who Pays" Principle

**Framework emphasized in class:**

1. **Would future buyers care?** (health/safety/legality/usability)
2. **Would future buyers learn?** (detected in ordinary DD?)
3. **Who controls/knows/can fix cheapest?**

**If (1) and (2) = yes ‚Üí Seller more likely to pay**
**If idiosyncratic preference ‚Üí Buyer pays**

### Transaction Toolkit

**All tools mentioned in prep were reinforced in class:**

| Tool | Purpose | Application |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Inspection contingency | Walk away if DD is bad | Bob needs this for tank/mold |
| Rep & warranty | Turn info into enforceable promise | Susan's weak "to best of knowledge" insufficient |
| Survival | Rep remains enforceable post-closing | Susan refused; major red flag |
| Escrow/holdback | Makes remedies realistic | Solution for pool systems timing |
| Price adjustment/credit | Changes economics without litigation | Alternative to seller fix |
| Third-party certification | Neutral expert decides | Prevents "everything is aesthetic" disputes |

## DOCTRINAL INSIGHTS

### Risk Allocation Principles

**Embedded in "who pays" framework:**

1. **Collective concern vs. idiosyncratic preference:**
   - Collective ‚Üí seller more likely to bear cost
   - Idiosyncratic ‚Üí buyer bears cost
   - Line: would "everyone" care (safety) vs. "you" care (aesthetics)

2. **Information access:**
   - Party with superior knowledge should bear verification cost
   - Or provide strong, surviving rep as alternative

3. **Control and capability:**
   - Party with control over property should fix
   - Or compensate buyer to fix post-closing

### Due Diligence Limitations

**Three types of DD barriers:**

1. **Physical impossibility** (pool systems in winter)
   - Solution: contingent closing, escrow, spring testing rights

2. **Information hiding** (mold paint-over)
   - Solution: professional standards, cannot rely on visual inspection alone

3. **Cost vs. risk** (balance \$1,000 scan vs. environmental liability)
   - Solution: cost-sharing, reimbursement if found

### Remedy Design

**Hierarchy of remedy strength:**

1. **Weakest:** Rep "to best of knowledge" + no survival
2. **Weak:** Seller fix (moral hazard - cheapest)
3. **Medium:** Price reduction/credit (buyer controls quality)
4. **Strong:** Escrow + professional standards + verification
5. **Strongest:** Closing condition with clear walk-away right
