**[But for Causation]{.mark}**

- Driving without your lights on at night and crash into house; **but for** your not having your lights off you would not have caused harm.

- **But-for cause** can be over-inclusive and under-inclusiveðŸ¡ªMSC, MNC

**[Multiple Sufficient Causes]{.mark}**

- Would not be But-for because injury would still happen

- One fire alone would have been sufficient to burn down house. The but-for test does not work because you cannot say that but-for one of the tortfeasor's fire, the house would not have burned down (since it would have burned down anyway)

**[Multiple Necessary Causes]{.mark}**

- Multiple people had to come together to cause tort

- One fire alone would not have been sufficient to cause the harm, and both fires were necessary. Neither are but-for causes (since but-for the fire there still needed to be another fire to bring down the house.)

**["Joint" Tortfeasors]{.mark}**

- Jointly agreeing to do this together;

**[Sequential Causes: Pre-emptive Causation]{.mark}**

- Neither party is a but-for cause, but the first to do the harm was the actual physical cause of the harm---first party's harm was **"Preemptive Cause"**

- First party is liable if negligent, and second party is not responsible even if negligent
