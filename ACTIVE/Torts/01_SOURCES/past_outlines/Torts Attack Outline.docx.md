**Torts Attack Outline**

**DUTY**

No duty or modified duty of reasonable care

- Factors in imposing duty: morality, foreseeability, extent of likely harms of D's conduct, burden of imposing duty, alternative ways of protecting P's interest, increased safety as result of imposing duty, deterrence, admin problems of enforcing duty

- Nonfeasance (autonomy) v. misfeasance

**Affirmative Obligation 13**

Where policy justices departing from no duty-rule: special relationship to victim, special relationship to perpetrator, innocent creation of risk

- No special relationship = no duty: Harper v. Herman (boat dive shallow)

- Non-negligent injury: Union Pacific Railway v. Capier, Maldanado v. Southern Pacific Transportation

- Non-negligent creation of risk: Simosen v. Thorin (utility pole), Menu v. Minor (cab), Resemer v. Barke (newly discovered danger)

- Special relationship social co-adventurers: Farwell v. Keaton

- Enabling Tort factors: foreseeability of harm, degree of certainty he suffered injury, closeness of connection btw D's conduct and injury, moral blame, policy of preventing future harm, consequences to community of imposing duty, cost of insurance for risk involved, social utility

  - Expand duty of care to unknown persons: Randi v. Muros (sexual assault recommendation)

  - Special relationship: Tarasoff v. Regents of UC (physican duty to warn), 10 minor cases 15

**Policy Basis No Duty**

D role in creating risk to P, sometimes policy reasons means no duty

- Limitless liability: Strauss v. Belle Realty (blackout fall), Asbestos, Moch v. Rensselaer Water (fire water utility)

- Duty towards identifiable group: Palka v. Servicemaster (wall fan)

- Negligent entrustment of dangerous instrumentality: Vince v. Wilson (grandnephew car)

**Landowner**

Traditional categories (trespasser, licensee, invitee) v. modern duty of reasonable care

Rules v. standards

- Social liability: Carter v. Kinney (traditional ice), Rowland v. Christian (sink faucet)

- Flagrant trespasser v. other trespassers: Bennett v. Napolitano (dog walker)

- Child trespasser, attractive nuisance: Holland v. Baltimore (freight train obvious)

- Property owners liable for foreseeable harm b/c of third parties: Kilne v. 1500 Mass Ave (tenant assault), Sargent v. Ross (rule)
